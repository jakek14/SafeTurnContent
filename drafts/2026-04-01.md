Best revenue year in company history.

Still couldn't make payroll.

Picture a manufacturing business doing $4M/year.

Owner takes an MCA in Q1 to fund a materials order. $120K. Daily payments: $900.

Business picks up. To fund more inventory, a second advance. Daily payments: $1,600 combined.

Then costs spike. A third MCA just to cover the gap.

Now $2,400 is leaving the account every business day. Automatically.

That's $52,800/month — before rent, labor, utilities, or anything else.

Revenue is higher than ever.

Cash is gone before it lands.

Here's what most owners don't know: MCA lenders can see each other's advances. They see it on the bank statements you submit when you apply.

They approve the third one anyway.

Because their money gets pulled first.

Your problems come second.

This is how the stacking trap works. Three separate daily withdrawals, each pulling at a different rate, creating a cash drain no amount of revenue can outrun.

The fix isn't more sales.

It's restructuring — renegotiating terms on each obligation, consolidating where possible, and aligning payments with actual cash flow.

Same total debt. Completely different math.

The business in this scenario doesn't have a revenue problem.

It has a structure problem. And structure problems have structural solutions.

If this pattern looks familiar, it doesn't get better by waiting.
