There's a window to fix your debt.

Most business owners don't realize it's closing.

Major banks are approving just 27% of small business loan applications right now.

SBA-backed loan volume is down 18% this year alone.

When traditional credit tightens, the options for restructuring existing debt shrink with it.

Here are 3 signs you're approaching the point where choices start to disappear:

1. You've been declined by a bank in the last 6-12 months.
Not because your business is failing. Because your existing obligation stack looks like a liability to every traditional lender who reviews your file.
Each new application makes the next one harder.

2. Debt payments are clearing before operations can fund.
Not just tight — structurally tight.
When lender withdrawals pull before payroll, rent, or inventory, you're not managing cash flow. You're rationing what's left.

3. You've taken new debt to cover an existing payment.
This is the clearest signal.
It means the structure itself is unsustainable — not temporarily stressed.

Here's what most business owners don't know:

The businesses that come through this don't avoid the problem. They address it while they still have options.

Restructuring is harder when you're three months deeper into the spiral.

It's harder when the bank has denied you twice and your obligation stack is tighter.

It's harder when the window is closed.
