Small business bankruptcies are at their highest point in over a decade.

The businesses filing today? Most of them were "managing" 12 months ago.

Here's how the slide usually happens — and where it can be stopped:

Stage 1: Stretched but functional.
One or two obligations. Payments are tight but you're covering them. You tell yourself revenue will catch up.

Stage 2: Filling gaps with more debt.
Cash flow gaps start appearing. You take another advance or credit line. Now 3+ payments are hitting the account every week. Payroll is still getting made — but barely.

This is the stage most owners don't recognize fast enough.

Stage 3: Triage mode.
You're using new credit to pay old credit. Daily withdrawals drain the account before customer payments land. Every week is a new emergency.

The critical window is Stage 2.

That's when restructuring is still relatively straightforward — terms can be renegotiated, payments can be realigned, and cash flow can open back up.

Most owners wait until Stage 3. By then, options are fewer, costs are higher, and the decisions are harder.

The data says more businesses are moving through this cycle right now than at any point since 2014.

If you recognize Stage 2, don't wait for Stage 3.
