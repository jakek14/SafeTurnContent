Before you take on another MCA or business loan — answer these 3 questions.

Most people skip them. That's how stacking starts.

63% of small business owners plan to seek more capital this year.

That number makes sense. Cash flow is tight and options feel limited.

But before adding another layer, these questions are worth slowing down for.

1. What is your total daily payment obligation right now?

Add up every daily or weekly withdrawal hitting your account — MCAs, term loans, revenue-based advances.

All of them. One number.

If you can't name it without opening a spreadsheet — that's worth pausing on.

2. What's your actual low point?

Not your monthly average. Your real floor — the lowest your bank balance hit in the last 30 days.

If that number is already tight before adding a new payment, more capital won't fix it. It will accelerate the problem.

3. Have you used new funding to cover an existing payment in the last 12 months?

This is the question most business owners answer quietly.

If yes — you're not solving a cash gap. You're cycling.

And every new advance makes the next exit harder.

The goal isn't to talk you out of capital.

It's to help you see whether more debt solves the problem — or just delays it.

When the answers to all 3 are uncomfortable, the issue usually isn't access to funding.

It's the structure underneath what you already have.
