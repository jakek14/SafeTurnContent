Most businesses that come to us aren't failing.

They're winning.

Record revenue. Growing team. New clients every month.

And completely out of cash.

Here's the thing nobody talks about:

You can build something real — and still watch it collapse — if the structure underneath doesn't match the revenue sitting on top of it.

A $5M/year business came to us last year. Growing 30% year-over-year for three straight years.

On paper? Thriving.

Three MCAs. Two term loans. Daily withdrawals totaling $2,100.

That's $42,000/month leaving the account before a single dollar touched payroll, rent, or operations.

They weren't mismanaging the business. They were building the business.

But every time revenue grew, the debt they took on to fuel that growth grew faster.

It was a race they couldn't win.

The reframe most owners need to hear:

Revenue is how big your business looks.
Structure is whether it can actually survive at that size.

Most owners are obsessed with the first number.

Almost nobody is watching the second.

If your business is growing but the pressure keeps getting worse — that's not a management failure.

That's a structural gap. And it widens the longer it goes unaddressed.

Fix the structure. Then let the growth mean something.
