Your business is profitable. Your bank account is empty.

These two things shouldn't coexist — and yet here we are.

Here are 3 signs your debt structure — not your business — is the actual problem:

1. You're current on all your payments, but you still can't make payroll.

Revenue is coming in. Every lender gets their cut. But by the time the daily and weekly withdrawals clear, there's not enough left to pay your people — or yourself.

That's not a revenue failure.

That's a payment structure consuming cash faster than the business can replenish it.

2. You can't tell someone exactly how much you're paying per month.

You have a rough sense. But between daily MCA pulls, bi-weekly loans, and monthly credit lines — all hitting the same account on different schedules — tracking it is nearly impossible.

If you can't see the full picture, you can't manage it.

3. You've taken new debt to cover existing payments. More than once.

It starts as a bridge. A short-term fix for a cash crunch.

But each new obligation adds another daily or weekly pull. The structure gets heavier. The breathing room gets smaller.

This is the cycle that takes down otherwise solid businesses.

Here's the part most people miss:

All three of these are structural problems — not operational ones.

The business isn't broken. The payment architecture wrapped around it is.

And payment structures can be rebuilt.
