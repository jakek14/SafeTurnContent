Most cash-strapped businesses don't have a spending problem.

They have an obligation problem.

There's a meaningful difference.

"Cut expenses" is the default advice when cash is tight.

And sometimes that's the right call.

But when your real problem is a debt structure pulling cash out faster than you can operate — trimming costs is like bailing water from a boat with a hole in the hull.

You're working hard. Doing everything right. The boat is still sinking.

Here's how to tell which problem you actually have:

Signs of a spending problem:
- Costs are high relative to your revenue
- Your P&L shows actual losses
- There are expenses you can identify as unnecessary

Signs of an obligation problem:
- Revenue is steady but cash is always short
- Daily or weekly lender pulls clear before payroll hits
- You've cut everything you can — and still can't breathe
- Your P&L looks fine on paper, but the bank account tells a different story

If the second list sounds familiar — cutting isn't the answer.

Restructuring is.

That means fewer payment obligations, better terms, and schedules that actually align with when cash comes in.

It's not about working harder or spending less.

It's about rebuilding the structure wrapped around your business so cash can flow the way it's supposed to.
