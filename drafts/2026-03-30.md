You haven't paid yourself in 3 months.

The business is still running. Clients are still paying. Revenue is up from last year.

But every dollar that comes in disappears before you can touch it.

You tell yourself it's temporary.

"Once this project closes, I'll catch up."
"Once that invoice clears, things will settle."

But the month turns over, and nothing settles.

Because the payments don't stop.

The MCA pulls every morning before you've had coffee. The credit line hits mid-month. The equipment loan comes out on the 1st. The second MCA you took to cover a gap pulls on a different schedule.

And somewhere in between all of it — there's nothing left for you.

Not for your salary. Not for a cushion. Not for a single week where you're not doing math in your head at 11pm.

This is what it looks like to be underpaid by your own business.

Not because you made bad decisions. Not because the business is failing.

But because a payment structure built piece by piece over 18 months wasn't designed to leave anything behind for the person running it.

The business runs. You don't.

If this is where you are — it's not a management problem.

It's a structural one. And those can be fixed.
