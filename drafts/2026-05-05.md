The SBA quietly closed one of the most common exit ramps for MCA debt last year.

Most business owners still don't know it happened.

For years, when a business got buried under MCA debt, SBA refinancing was one of the cleaner ways out.

Take the advances, roll them into an SBA loan. Longer term. Monthly payments instead of daily. A chance to breathe.

That changed in June 2025. The SBA eliminated MCA debt from eligible refinancing uses.

This matters more than most people realize.

Business owners carrying multiple advances — daily payments stacking, bank statements deteriorating — are now operating without one of the key pressure release valves that used to exist.

Banks aren't filling the gap. Major institutions approve roughly 27% of small business loan applications overall. That number drops fast when the account reflects daily MCA pulls and tight cash flow.

A business juggling two or three advances with a bruised financial profile isn't getting a traditional loan to clean it up.

What this means practically:

The exit strategy has to look different now.

Restructuring directly with the MCA lenders — negotiating modified terms, extended payback, reduced balances — is increasingly the primary lever available.

Not because it's the ideal path.

Because most of the others have closed.

If you're carrying MCA debt and waiting for a cleaner exit to open up — it's worth understanding what options are actually still on the table.

The landscape changed.
