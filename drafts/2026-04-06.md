Business bankruptcies hit their highest level since 2014 last quarter.

These weren't businesses that gave up.

Most of them were growing.

Here's the part nobody's saying out loud:

Revenue growth doesn't fix a broken payment structure.

When you have 3 or 4 obligations pulling from the same operating account — daily, weekly, in different amounts — more revenue just means more cash flowing in and flowing right back out.

The conventional advice: cut expenses. Grow faster. Work harder.

That advice works when the problem is performance.

It doesn't work when the problem is structure.

Picture two businesses, same revenue, same expenses, same owner effort.

One operates fine. Pays bills on time. Has cash left over at the end of the month.

The other is in survival mode every week. Owner works longer hours. Revenue keeps climbing.

Account stays thin.

The difference? Timing.

Not the amount of debt — when it hits.

When debt payments hit relative to when cash actually lands.

That one variable — not effort, not talent, not revenue — is what separates the business that breathes from the one that doesn't.

If you're working harder than ever and your account still can't keep up —

It's not a performance problem.

It's a structure problem. And that has a completely different solution.
