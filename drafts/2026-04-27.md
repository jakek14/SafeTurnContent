SMB bankruptcies hit a 15-year high in 2025.

Most didn't fail because of bad revenue.

They failed because the warning signs showed up months before the crisis — and nobody recognized them.

Here are 3 patterns worth knowing:

1. You're borrowing to cover what you already borrowed.

When new debt is the only way to stay current on existing payments, the structure has already broken. The new capital isn't solving anything — it's deferring the problem, with a cost attached.

2. You're tracking cash flow after the fact.

Most businesses at risk look at their bank balance to understand what happened — not what's coming. No forward view. No 30-60 day picture of what's due.

That's not cash management. That's cleanup.

3. Banks are saying no, and you're turning to faster capital.

Traditional lenders tightened standards sharply in 2025. When they pull back, the remaining options are more accessible — and far more expensive. A 1.3 factor rate on a 6-month MCA runs roughly 60% APR. Stack two of them and the effective cost can top 100%.

The speed of approval doesn't change what it costs.

None of these is fatal on its own.

All three together — in a business doing real revenue — is a structure problem.

And structure can be fixed. But it's a lot easier to fix before the wall hits.
