It wasn't.

Because now you had daily pulls. A repayment structure built to collect fast, not help you sustain.

Most business owners who end up buried under 2, 3, or 4 lenders don't trace it back to bad judgment.

They trace it back to the first yes.

Major banks approve roughly 27% of small business loan applications. The other 73% get redirected — toward alternative lenders who say yes fast, fund in 24 hours, and spend little time on whether your cash flow can actually carry it.

Alternative lenders approve 70–85% of applicants.

That accessibility isn't charity. It's the business model.

For an owner under pressure, fast approval feels like the right answer.

It isn't. It's the beginning of a structure you'll spend the next 12–18 months trying to unwind.

Getting approved doesn't mean your problem is solved.

It means the real problem — how you service what you just took on — just started.

The goal isn't to get approved. It's to get to a payment structure your business can actually sustain.

Those two things are not the same.

If the approval made things harder to manage, not easier — you weren't saved.

You were just loaded with different weight.
