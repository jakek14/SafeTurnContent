The goal isn't to pay off the debt.

It's to stop the daily bleed.

Most business owners under debt pressure are obsessed with one number: the total balance.

And it makes sense. That number feels like the enemy.

But here's what actually determines whether your business survives the next 90 days:

Not how much you owe.

How fast it's leaving.

There's a real difference between having $350K in outstanding debt — and having $350K in debt that pulls $400/day from your operating account before you've made a single sale.

One is a number on a balance sheet.

The other is a cash flow leak that compounds every week you let it run.

When multiple obligations — MCAs, term loans, vendor arrangements — all draw from the same account on the same schedule, the math stops being manageable.

The business isn't failing because it owes too much.

It's failing because the structure of when and how it pays is quietly choking operations.

Restructuring addresses exactly this.

Not by wiping out balances — but by changing the timing, the pace, and the priority of what leaves the account.

That's the shift most people never get pointed toward.

If the balance feels manageable but the daily grind feels impossible — that gap is exactly what restructuring is designed to close.
