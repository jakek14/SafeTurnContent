Revenue is up.

But the account is still empty every Friday.

This is one of the most disorienting things a business owner can experience — and it's more common right now than most people realize.

Here's what's happening:

Revenue growth doesn't fix a broken payment structure.

If you have daily MCA pulls, weekly loan payments, and stacked obligations — more revenue just means more cash flowing into a system that's already draining it out.

The money hits the account. Then it's gone before you can use it.

So you push harder. Land more clients. Close more deals. Revenue climbs.

But the debt schedule doesn't respond to that. It keeps pulling, every business day, on its own timeline.

The business isn't failing.

The structure underneath is pulling cash out faster than the business can retain it.

Those are two completely different problems — and they have two completely different solutions.

More revenue solves a revenue problem.

But if obligations are consuming cash the moment it arrives, that's not a revenue problem. That's a structure problem.

And a structure problem requires restructuring — not more sales.

This is why businesses doing $3M, $5M, even $10M a year can still feel like they're drowning.

Revenue is fine. Structure isn't.

If that description fits where you are right now, the path forward isn't more hustle. It's fixing what's underneath.
