The more debt you take on trying to fix a cash flow problem, the less likely a bank is to help you next time.

It's called the debt spiral.

And most business owners don't realize they're in it.

Here's how it plays out:

Cash gets tight. You apply for a bank loan.

Bank reviews your financials. Sees existing obligations. Passes.

In 2024, 41% of small businesses denied bank financing were told the reason: too much existing debt — nearly double the rate from 2021.

So you turn to the only lender who says yes.

An MCA. An online lender. Fast money, expensive terms.

It bridges the gap.

But now you carry more debt than before.

Next time cash gets tight, banks say no even faster.

You're not getting rejected because your business is bad.

You're getting rejected because your existing obligation stack looks like a liability to every traditional lender.

More credit isn't the solution to a credit problem.

The real exit: restructure what you already owe. Renegotiate terms. Extend timelines. Align payments with your actual cash flow cycle.

When the obligation stack gets lighter, lenders see something different.

Options reopen.

But it starts with one counterintuitive move:

Stop chasing more credit. Start fixing the structure of what you already have.
