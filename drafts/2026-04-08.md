Getting another loan to fix a debt problem is like calling your credit card company for a cash advance to pay your rent.

It feels like relief. It's actually a trap.

Right now, more than half of small businesses that apply for financing don't get everything they asked for.

Banks are tightening. Credit is harder to access than it's been in years.

Yet the first advice most owners get when they're struggling is: "Go get more capital."

Here's what that misses:

When your debt structure is already broken, more credit doesn't fix it.

It adds one more payment to the same account being drained by the others.

The problem isn't how much capital you can access.

It's how your existing obligations are structured — and when they're hitting your cash.

Picture three lenders pulling from the same operating account. Different rates, different days, different terms. No coordination.

Adding a fourth doesn't help. It accelerates the problem.

The businesses that stabilize don't start by adding more capital. They start by restructuring what's already there.

Fewer obligations. Better timing. Payment terms aligned to actual cash flow.

Same total debt — completely different math.

The answer is almost never another loan.

It's almost always a structural fix.

If "go get more financing" isn't working, it's not because you're failing.

It's because you're following advice designed for a different problem.
