45% of small business owners skip their own paycheck to keep the business running.

That's not discipline.

That's a structure problem wearing the mask of sacrifice.

Here's the reframe most owners never make:

Skipping your salary doesn't fix the business.

It subsidizes a broken payment structure — temporarily.

When cash gets tight, owners cut themselves first. Then growth spend. Then team perks. Then marketing.

But the MCA withdrawal still hits at 8am. The term loan still pulls on the 1st. The obligations don't know — or care — that you haven't paid yourself in 90 days.

So nothing actually changes in the structure.

You just absorb the pain into your personal life.

The real question isn't "how do I stretch this month's cash further?"

It's "why is cash disappearing before I can use it in the first place?"

Those are two completely different questions with two completely different answers.

One answer keeps you grinding and sacrificing indefinitely.

The other answer restructures where the money goes — and puts you back in control.

If you haven't paid yourself in 60+ days, the business doesn't have a hustle problem.

It has a structure problem that's quietly bleeding into your personal finances.

That's fixable. But not by working harder.
