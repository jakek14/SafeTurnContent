Commercial bankruptcies jumped 67% in February.

Most of those businesses weren't bad businesses.

They were good businesses with a broken payment structure — and not enough runway.

Here's what that looks like before it becomes a statistic:

Real revenue. Real employees. Real clients who love what you do.

But you're carrying 3, 4, maybe 5 lenders all pulling from the same operating account.

A daily MCA withdrawal at 8am. A weekly term loan on Monday. A line of credit payment on the 15th.

And every morning, the first thing you do isn't check sales.

It's check the balance.

"Do I have enough to cover today?"

That's the shift nobody talks about.

When you stop thinking about growth and start thinking about survival, everything still looks fine on the outside.

Customers have no idea. Employees still show up. Revenue is still coming in.

But you're doing math around the clock.

Moving money between accounts the night before a withdrawal. Watching the balance drop while waiting for an invoice to clear. Covering payroll by the skin of your teeth.

This isn't a hustle problem.

It's a structure problem that's slowly consumed your business — and your life.

The jump in commercial bankruptcies isn't random.

It's what happens when structure problems go unaddressed long enough.

The structure can be fixed.

But not by waiting.
