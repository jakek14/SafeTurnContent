# Safe Turn Advisory — Voice Guide

## Who We Sound Like

A calm, experienced operator who has seen hundreds of businesses in debt trouble and knows exactly what's going wrong — and how to fix it.

Think: the friend who happens to be a financial strategist. Not a suit. Not a salesman. Someone who gets it.

## Tone Spectrum

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|--------|------------|
| Direct | Aggressive |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Empathetic | Pitying |
| Knowledgeable | Academic |
| Slightly contrarian | Edgy for the sake of it |
| Calm under pressure | Detached |
| Operator-minded | Corporate |

## Language Rules

### Use
- "You" and "your" — talk directly to the reader
- Short, punchy sentences
- Plain English — 8th grade reading level
- Concrete examples and numbers
- Metaphors from business operations (not finance jargon)

### Avoid
- "We" and "our" in hooks (save for authority sections)
- Buzzwords: synergy, leverage, optimize, disrupt, scale, pivot
- Finance jargon: amortization, restructuring vehicles, debt instruments (unless explaining)
- Emojis as bullet points
- ALL CAPS for emphasis (use line breaks instead)
- Exclamation marks (calm confidence, not excitement)

## The Bar Test

Before posting, ask: "Would I say this to a stressed-out business owner sitting next to me at a bar?"

If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it.
If it sounds like a LinkedIn guru, delete it.
If it sounds like something a real person would say — post it.

## Example Voice Comparisons

**Too corporate:**
"Our comprehensive debt restructuring solutions enable businesses to achieve sustainable cash flow optimization."

**Too casual:**
"Bro your MCA payments are eating you alive lol"

**Just right:**
"You're making money. But every dollar that comes in is already spoken for. That's not a revenue problem. That's a structure problem."

## Hook Voice Examples

**Data drop:** "73% of businesses that take on an MCA never fully recover their cash flow. Not because the business failed — because the payments never stopped."

**Story:** "A restaurant owner called us last month. $2.4M in revenue. Couldn't make payroll. Not because business was slow — because 4 different lenders were pulling from the same account every day."

**Contrarian:** "Your accountant sees your P&L. They don't see your cash flow. That's why their advice isn't working."

**Pattern interrupt:** "Stop applying for another loan. That's not what you need right now."
